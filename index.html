The story
The story

I did not start out as a coach.
I started out as the client who needed one.

For a long time I trained the way most people do. Hard, inconsistent, chasing the next thing. Some of it worked. A lot of it did not.

The shift came when I stopped looking for shortcuts and started paying attention to what actually compounds. Sleep. Tension. Position. Recovery. The boring fundamentals that nobody sells you because they cannot be packaged.

Coaching, for me, is the work of getting other people to the same place faster than I got there. Without the years of wasted effort. Without the noise.

What I believe

Three things I will not compromise on.

01

Sustainable over extreme.

Anyone can crush a client for six weeks. The real test is whether the work is still standing in a year. I coach for the long arc, not the before-and-after.

02

Strength as the foundation.

Strength is what every other quality is built on. Conditioning, aesthetics, longevity, resilience. Skip it and the rest is sand.

03

The body is a long project.

The version of you in ten years is being built right now, in small decisions. The job of a coach is to make those decisions easier, not louder.

Who this is for

I work with a specific kind of person.

Adults who have trained before. Who have hit a ceiling, lost the thread, or want a method that takes them seriously.

People who are done with gimmicks. Who do not need motivation, they need a plan that fits their life and a coach who pays attention. Who would rather get one thing right for a decade than ten things wrong for a season.
